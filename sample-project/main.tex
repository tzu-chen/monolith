\begin{abstract}
We study a class of mean field games where the
representative agent's dynamics are governed by a
stochastic differential equation with state-dependent
volatility coupled to the population measure.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Mean field game theory, initiated by Lasry--Lions and
Huang--Malham\'e--Caines, provides a tractable framework
for studying Nash equilibria in large-population
stochastic differential games. In this paper, we extend
the classical MFG framework to incorporate stochastic
volatility that depends on the population distribution.

\section{Mean Field Model}
Consider a continuum of agents $i \in [0,1]$
whose state $X^i_t$ evolves according to:
\begin{equation}
  dX^i_t = b(X^i_t, \mu_t, \alpha^i_t)\,dt
  + \sigma(X^i_t, \mu_t)\,dW^i_t
\end{equation}
where $\mu_t \in \meas(\mathbb{R}^d)$ denotes
the population distribution at time $t$, and
$\alpha^i_t$ is the control process chosen by
agent $i$.

\section{Nash Equilibrium}
A Nash equilibrium in this setting consists of a
pair $(\hat{\alpha}, \hat{\mu})$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Given the population flow $\hat{\mu}$, the
        control $\hat{\alpha}$ is optimal for the
        representative agent.
  \item The flow $\hat{\mu}$ is consistent with the
        distribution of the optimally controlled state.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Numerical Results}
We present numerical solutions obtained via a
finite-difference scheme applied to the coupled
HJB--FP system. The convergence rate is observed
to be of order $\mathcal{O}(h^2)$ in the mesh size $h$.
