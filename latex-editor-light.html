Mean Field Games with Stochastic Volatility
J. Author
March 2026
Abstract
We study a class of mean field games where the representative agent's dynamics are governed by a stochastic differential equation with state-dependent volatility coupled to the population measure.
1  Introduction
Mean field game theory, initiated by Lasry–Lions and Huang–Malhamé–Caines, provides a tractable framework for studying Nash equilibria in large-population stochastic differential games…
2  Mean Field Model
Consider a continuum of agents i ∈ [0,1] whose state Xit evolves according to:
dXit = b(Xit, μt, αit) dt + σ(Xit, μt) dWit
where μt ∈ 𝒫(ℝd) denotes the population distribution at time t, and αit is the control process chosen by agent i.
